1- Functional purposes (e.g., requests)

Communicating mainly to get something or make something happen.

Examples:

  • Asking for food or a toy
  • Pointing to a desired object
  • Saying “more” to get another turn or more of something

3- Mixed

Communicating for both functional and expressive reasons.

Examples:

  • Asking for a snack and also telling someone “I’m happy”
  • Pointing to a toy and showing excitement about it

5- Expressive purposes

Communicating mainly to share feelings, thoughts, or ideas.

Examples:

  • Saying “I love you”
  • Telling a story
  • Showing excitement or frustration without requesting anything
